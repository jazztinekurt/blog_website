Technology in Everyday Life

By Jazztine Kurt Ramos

Technology has become a very important part of our everyday life, even though we don’t always notice it. From the moment we wake up to the time we go to sleep, we depend on technology in many small ways. As a second-year student, I see how much technology affects not only my studies but also how people communicate and live.

One of the biggest changes technology has brought is in communication. Earlier, people mostly depended on phone calls or face-to-face conversations. Now, with social media apps, messaging platforms, and video calls, we can talk to anyone around the world within seconds. For students, this is very helpful because we can discuss assignments, attend online classes, and share study materials easily.

Technology has also changed the education system. Online learning platforms, digital notes, and educational videos make learning more flexible and interesting. If we don’t understand a topic in class, we can search for explanations online and learn at our own pace. During exams and projects, technology helps us research topics faster and present our work in a better way.

Another important area where technology plays a role is entertainment. Many students spend their free time watching videos, listening to music, or playing online games. While this can be relaxing, it can also become a distraction if not used properly. Because of this, it is important for students to learn how to manage their time and use technology wisely.

However, technology also has some disadvantages. Excessive use of mobile phones and laptops can affect our health, such as causing eye strain and reducing physical activity. There are also concerns about privacy and over-dependence on digital devices. These issues remind us that technology should be used as a tool, not as something we completely rely on.

In conclusion, technology has made life easier and more convenient, especially for students. It helps us learn, communicate, and stay informed. At the same time, we must be careful about how we use it. If used in a balanced way, technology can be a powerful support in both education and daily life.
